⚝ Getting Thing Done

Goal: Offer a solution

The Causative
Use the causative to express the idea that one person persuades or "causes" another person to do something.

get + an object + an infinite verb

I'll get the waiter to correct the check.

have + an object + a base form verb

It express the idea that one person directs another to do something.

We had them bring breakfast to our room.

Passive Causative
The passive causative focuses on the object rather than the subject of the sentence.

get/have + an object + an past participle verb

I get my car fixed at my uncles garage.

We're having a new house built now.

Historic Disasters
Armero tragedy

The Armero tragedy was a natural disaster resulting from the eruption of the Nevado del Ruiz volcano on Wednesday , November 13, 1985 , which affected the departments of Caldas and Tolima , Colombia . After sixty-nine years of inactivity, the eruption took nearby towns by surprise, despite the fact that the Government had received warnings from multiple volcanological organizations since the appearance of the first signs of volcanic activity in September 1985. he town of Armero , located just under 50 km from the volcano, was hit by these lahars, killing more than 20,000 of its 29,000 inhabitants.
